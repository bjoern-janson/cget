# 05 — Agency

> *Agency is the capacity to intentionally steer counterfactual reality while preserving coherent self-transformation.*

---

# Overview

Within CGET, **agency** is distinct from intelligence.

An intelligent system may possess an accurate world model yet remain unable to influence its environment.

Likewise, a system may act upon the world without understanding it.

CGET defines agency as the bridge between representation and action.

An agent possesses agency to the extent that it can intentionally transform reachable futures into selected futures through executable policy.

---

# The Core Problem

A causal model defines what is **possible**.

A policy determines what is **actualized**.

These are fundamentally different.

The central agency problem is therefore:

> **How much of the reachable future space can an agent intentionally control?**

---

# Reachable vs. Steerable Futures

The epistemic engine defines

[
\mathcal W_{\text{reachable}}(\mathcal G),
]

the set of futures permitted by the discovered causal language.

However,

not every reachable future can be intentionally selected.

Define

[
\mathcal W_{\text{steerable}}(\mathcal G,\Pi)
\subseteq
\mathcal W_{\text{reachable}}(\mathcal G),
]

where

(\Pi)

is the available policy space.

Agency concerns the relationship between these two sets.

---

# Counterfactual Steering

CGET defines **Counterfactual Steering Bandwidth**

[
\mathcal A_{\text{control}}
===========================

\frac
{
|\mathcal W_{\text{steerable}}|
}
{
|\mathcal W_{\text{reachable}}|
}.
]

Conceptually,

* low values indicate little influence,
* high values indicate strong intentional control.

The metric separates genuine agency from passive participation in complex dynamics.

---

# Why Steering Matters

Many physical systems produce enormous numbers of future states.

Examples include

* hurricanes,
* forest fires,
* collapsing civilizations,
* chaotic particle systems.

These systems generate tremendous future complexity.

They do **not** possess agency.

The difference is not the number of reachable futures.

The difference is intentional steering.

---

# Policy Space

Policies define executable strategies

[
\pi
\in
\Pi.
]

A policy maps observations into actions.

Within CGET,

policy quality is determined not by accumulated reward,

but by its ability to

* preserve viability,
* expand controllable futures,
* remain executable.

---

# Agency Kernel

Not every viable system possesses agency.

Define the **Agency Kernel**

[
\mathcal K_{\text{agency}}.
]

Membership requires

* remaining inside the viability kernel,
* possessing executable policy,
* maintaining intentional control over future trajectories.

Conceptually,

[
\mathcal K_{\text{agency}}
\subseteq
\mathcal K_{\text{viable}}.
]

Every agent must remain viable.

Not every viable object is an agent.

---

# The Agency Spectrum

Agency exists on a continuum.

## Stage 0 — Chaos

No coherent identity.

No policy.

No intentional steering.

Examples include

* explosions,
* hurricanes,
* uncontrolled chain reactions.

---

## Stage 1 — Passive Objects

Stable identity.

No policy.

No steering.

Examples include

* rocks,
* crystals,
* passive artifacts.

---

## Stage 2 — Fixed Controllers

Limited policy.

Rigid behavior.

Minimal adaptation.

Examples include

* thermostats,
* PID controllers,
* simple feedback systems.

---

## Stage 3 — Adaptive Agents

Learnable policy.

Expanding causal model.

Self-modification.

Examples include

* animals,
* humans,
* advanced artificial agents.

---

# Agency and Intelligence

Agency and intelligence reinforce one another.

Better representations improve steering.

Better steering generates better observations.

This creates a recursive feedback loop.

```text
Representation
      │
      ▼
Prediction
      │
      ▼
Action
      │
      ▼
Environmental Change
      │
      ▼
New Information
      │
      └──────────────► Better Representation
```

Neither component alone produces sustained intelligence.

---

# Computational Leverage

Agency determines how effectively computational knowledge can be translated into physical influence.

A perfect causal model without action produces observation.

A perfect actuator without understanding produces randomness.

Agency emerges from their combination.

---

# Agency and Self-Modification

The strongest form of agency is recursive.

Rather than modifying only the environment,

the system also modifies

* its representations,
* its policies,
* its execution strategies,
* its own internal architecture.

However,

self-modification remains constrained by

* identity continuity,
* transition stability,
* viability preservation.

Otherwise,

recursive improvement becomes recursive self-destruction.

---

# Failure Modes

The agency framework rejects several pathological cases.

---

## Passive Persistence

Long-term survival without intentional control.

High viability.

Zero agency.

---

## Random Exploration

Large behavioral diversity without directed steering.

Many reachable futures.

Few controllable futures.

---

## Illusory Control

The agent believes it influences outcomes but lacks actual causal leverage.

Low steering bandwidth.

---

## Runaway Optimization

Increasing influence accompanied by loss of core identity.

Agency expands while coherent self-transformation collapses.

---

# Relationship to the Epistemic Engine

The epistemic engine answers

> What futures are possible?

Agency answers

> Which futures can I intentionally realize?

---

# Relationship to the Normative Engine

The normative engine answers

> Which futures should be pursued?

Agency answers

> Which desirable futures are actually controllable?

---

# Structural Role in CGET

Agency connects the two halves of the architecture.

```text
Epistemic Engine
      │
      ▼
Reachable Futures
      │
      ▼
Agency
      │
```

agency becomes blind.

---

# Summary

Within CGET,

agency is the capacity to intentionally transform reachable futures into selected futures through executable policy.

It is measured not by how many futures exist,

but by how many of those futures remain under coherent control.

Agency therefore represents the operational bridge between understanding reality and intentionally shaping it while preserving the conditions necessary for continued self-transformation.
